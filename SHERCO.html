A hands-on mechanical project: restoring, upgrading, and customizing my Sherco 50cc motorcycle.

Engine & Performance Upgrades

I replaced the original cylinder with a high-performance Bidalot 70cc kit, upgraded the carburetor for better fuel delivery, and installed a racing exhaust. These modifications significantly increased the engine's power and responsiveness.

Chassis, Suspension & Brakes

I overhauled the suspension for improved handling and replaced worn brake components to ensure safety. The frame was reinforced and repainted, giving the bike a fresh look and better durability for off-road use.

Electrical & Aesthetic Customization

I installed a new wiring harness, LED lighting, and custom graphics to personalize the bike. These changes improved reliability and gave the Sherco a unique, modern appearance.

Rear Wheel and Spring Integration

The rear wheel design involved advanced modeling techniques. Using sweep operations and parametric laws, I crafted helicoidal springs with varying radius to serve both aesthetic and structural purposes. Multiple B√©zier curves and multi-section surfaces shaped the wheel, while circular patterns were used to duplicate the springs evenly.

Front Fairing and Unicorn Horn

The front fairing includes a sculpted unicorn head and horn, achieved with complex surface operations and sweep functions. Defining multiple planes for curve projections was crucial. I also added an 80mm pocket to embed a headlight, blending design with utility.

Front Fender Shaping

The fender design required accurate alignment with the wheel. I used splines and multi-surface tools to wrap the geometry around the tire and brake disk. Precise transformations were needed to properly align the given profile to the tilted orientation of the wheel.
